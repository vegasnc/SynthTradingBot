================================================================================
PART 1: WHAT IS THIS TRYING TO DO?
================================================================================

In simple words:
- You trade US stocks during the day (day trading).
- You use a fixed set of rules to decide WHEN to buy and WHEN to sell.
- In the document, the goal is to build a computer program (bot) that does

So "this trading" = rule-based US stock day trading, with the option to
automate it.


================================================================================
PART 2: HOW DOES THIS TRADING WORK? (STEP BY STEP)
================================================================================

2.1  WHICH STOCKS CAN YOU TRADE?
--------------------------------
Only stocks that pass these filters (the "universe"):

  - US stocks only
  - Stock price above $5.00
  - Today’s volume (shares traded) is more than 1 million
  - Regular trading hours only (no pre-market or after-hours for this strategy)

So you are not trading every stock; only liquid, reasonably priced US names.


2.2  HOW MUCH MONEY PER TRADE?
------------------------------
- Your account is split into 10% pieces.
- Example: $100,000 account → up to 10 positions, each about $10,000.
- So you never put more than 10% of your account into one stock.
- In the document: no margin (cash only).


2.3  WHEN DO YOU ENTER A TRADE? (ENTRY RULES)
---------------------------------------------
You buy a stock only when ALL of these are true:

  1. On the PREVIOUS trading day (daily chart):
     - RSI with 2 periods (RSI(2)) closed below 10.
     So the stock was very oversold yesterday.

  2. TODAY, on the 5-minute chart:
     - A 5-minute candle CLOSES above yesterday’s daily high.
     So price breaks above the prior day’s high on the 5m chart.

In short: yesterday was very oversold (RSI(2) < 10), and today price breaks
above yesterday’s high on a 5-minute close. That’s your entry signal.


2.4  WHEN DO YOU EXIT? (EXIT RULES)
-----------------------------------
- You use a TRAILING STOP based on volatility (ATR).
- ATR period: 14, on the 5-minute chart.
- Distance: 2 × ATR (5-minute).
- The stop is recalculated every 5 minutes when a new bar closes; you only
  tighten the stop (never move it further away).
- So if price goes up, your stop follows; if price drops to the stop level,
  you are sold and the position is closed.

Additional rules from the document:
- Close ALL remaining positions by 3:45 PM ET.
- Do not open NEW trades after 3:00 PM ET.
- One trade per stock per day (no re-entry if stopped out).


2.5  IF TOO MANY STOCKS QUALIFY
-------------------------------
If more than 10 names meet the entry rules but you only have 10 slots:
- Rank them by BETA (how much they move vs the market).
- Take the highest Beta first until you fill your 10 positions.


================================================================================
PART 3: HOW TO DO THIS TRADING (MANUAL VS AUTOMATED)
================================================================================

3.1  MANUAL TRADING
-------------------
You can do the same logic by hand:

  1. Before the open: get a list of US stocks with price > $5 and volume
     > 1M (from your broker or a scanner).
  2. For each, check: did yesterday’s daily RSI(2) close below 10? Note
     yesterday’s high.
  3. During the day: watch 5-minute charts. When a 5m bar CLOSES above
     yesterday’s high, that’s an entry.
  4. Size each trade to ~10% of your account; no margin.
  5. Put a trailing stop at 2 × 14-period ATR (5m), and update it every
     5 minutes (tighten only).
  6. Close everything by 3:45 PM ET; no new entries after 3:00 PM ET.

You need:
- A broker that lets you trade US stocks (e.g. Interactive Brokers).
- Real-time or delayed data for daily + 5-minute charts.
- A platform or spreadsheet to compute RSI(2), prior day high, and 5m ATR.


3.2  AUTOMATED TRADING (WHAT THE DOCUMENT DESCRIBES)
----------------------------------------------------
The JD.md file describes building a BOT that:

  - Scans for stocks that pass the filters (price, volume).
  - Computes yesterday’s RSI(2) and prior day high.
  - Watches 5-minute bars and enters when 5m close > prior day high.
  - Sizes positions at 10% of account, ranks by Beta if oversubscribed.
  - Places and manages 2×ATR(14) trailing stops (recalculated every 5m,
    tighten only).
  - Flattens all positions by 3:45 PM ET and stops new entries at 3:00 PM ET.
  - Runs on a server (VPS) with a small dashboard to monitor positions and
    orders.

So "how to do this trading" in the document = run this logic automatically
via a Python-based system connected to Interactive Brokers (and optionally
QuantConnect or similar).

PART 4: WHAT YOU SHOULD DO TO START THIS TRADING
================================================================================

4.1  IF YOU WANT TO TRADE THIS STRATEGY MANUALLY
-----------------------------------------------
  1. Open a brokerage account that supports US equities (e.g. Interactive
     Brokers).
  2. Ensure you have market data for US stocks (daily + intraday 5-minute).
  3. Learn how to compute RSI(2) on the daily chart and how to read
     "prior day high."
  4. Use a charting platform (e.g. TradingView, or your broker’s) that
     supports 5-minute charts and ATR(14).
  5. Start with a watchlist of liquid US stocks (price > $5, volume > 1M).
  6. Paper trade first: write down signals and track how the strategy would
     have performed without risking real money.
  7. When comfortable, trade with small size (e.g. one 10% position) and
     strictly follow the entry/exit and time rules above.


4.2  IF YOU WANT TO AUTOMATE IT (AS IN THE DOCUMENT)
----------------------------------------------------
  1. Have (or open) an Interactive Brokers account (paper account first).
  2. Get IBKR market data subscriptions needed for US stocks.
  3. Decide who builds the bot: you (Python + IBKR API) or a contractor (as
     in the chat in JD.md).
  4. If using a contractor: use the JD.md content as the specification;
     the proposal in the file (scanner, execution, trailing stops, dashboard,
     VPS, paper then live) is the roadmap.
  5. Run in PAPER TRADING first until results and behavior match the rules.
  6. Then deploy to a VPS, connect to live IBKR (with real money only when
     you are satisfied), and use the dashboard to monitor and control the bot.


4.3  THINGS TO DECIDE BEFORE GOING LIVE

- Daily max loss “kill switch” (optional but recommended).
- Exact ATR period and multiplier if you want to test variations.
- Whether you allow re-entry in the same stock after a stop-out (in the
  document: no re-entry).
